# NOVANA — National Environmental Monitoring

The National Monitoring Programme for Water and Nature, now in its 4th generation (2023–2027). Run jointly by Miljøstyrelsen and Aarhus University (DCE).

## What NOVANA measures

- Nitrogen/phosphorus transport to coastal waters (time series since 1990)
- Oxygen depletion in inner Danish waters
- Groundwater pesticides, PFAS (including TFA)
- Air quality
- Hazardous substances (MFS)
- Stream fauna (DVFI index)
- Lake biology, coastal biology
- Habitat conditions

## Eight specialized data centers (fagdatacentre)

1. Marine areas
2. Freshwater streams
3. Lakes
4. Land/terrestrial
5. Species and habitats
6. Atmosphere
7. Groundwater (via GEUS)
8. Point sources (via Miljøstyrelsen)

## Publication cycle

- Raw data → quality control at data centers → annual sub-reports → comprehensive summary
- Latest: "Vandmiljø og Natur 2024" published December 8, 2025 (DCE SR687)
- **~12 month lag** between monitoring year and publication

## Key metrics from NOVANA

| Metric | Current value | Target | Trend |

| Nitrogen transport to coast | ~52,000 tonnes (2022) | 38,400 tonnes/year | Declining from ~100,000 (1990) |

## NKMv2025 — National Nitrogen Model

Published 2025 by DCE + GEUS. Key component:
- High-resolution nitrogen retention maps (10×10m)
- Calculates how much nitrogen is retained when specific parcels are converted
- **Used by MARS** for automatic nitrogen effect calculations
- Used by SGAV for setting emission quotas from 2027

## NOVANA data access

- Raw data: Through Danmarks Miljøportal systems (VanDa, Kemidata, Naturdata)
- Reports: DCE scientific reports (PDF) — freely downloadable
- LOOP programme: 5 representative agricultural catchments for nitrogen leaching
- Potential nitrogen emissions map: Released Nov 2025 via Danmarks Miljøportal

## Relevance for the tracker

NOVANA provides the **outcome layer** — environmental status data that shows whether land transformation actually improves water/nature quality. It does NOT track implementation progress.

**Key limitation**: Annual publication with ~12 month lag means the tracker can show "administrative success" (many hectares in "anlagt" status in MARS) while ecological reality documented by DCE remains invisible for years.
