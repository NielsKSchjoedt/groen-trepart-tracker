# Governance Structure

## Hierarchy

```
Politisk Forum for Arealudtag (PFA)
  ├── Minister, L&F chair, DN president, KL representative
  │
National Steering Group
  │
Taskforce for Arealudtag (monthly status meetings)
  │
23 Local Green Tripartites (lokale treparter)
  ├── Organized around 23 main river basins (hovedvandoplande)
  ├── Lead municipality (formandskommune) + secretariat municipality
  ├── Produce transformation plans (omlægningsplaner) in MARS
  └── Plans are structured around 37 hydrological coastal water groups
      │
98 Municipalities
  ├── Primary project implementers
  ├── Register/update projects in MARS
  ├── Principle-adopt transformation plans (principvedtagelse)
  └── Serve as project owners for water/climate projects
```

## The 23 local tripartites

Each local tripartite corresponds to one of Denmark's 23 main river basins (hovedvandoplande). They are the legally responsible planning units for transformation progress.

Key responsibilities:
- Prepare cross-municipal transformation plans in MARS
- Produce coastal water group reports (kystvandgrupperapporter) for each of the 37 hydrological groups within their area
- Coordinate landowner engagement
- Submit plans for SGAV verification

## Process flow for plan adoption

1. Local tripartites create sketch projects (skitseprojekter) in MARS
2. Coastal water group reports are produced and published in MARS
3. SGAV receives verification request (from Sep 2025)
4. SGAV verifies that combined plans meet nitrogen/area targets
5. Municipal councils principle-adopt the parts within their boundary (deadline: end 2025)
6. Implementation proceeds via concrete projects (pre-investigation → realization → completed)

## MARS as monitoring backbone

MARS (Multifunktionel ArealRegistrering System) calculates three core effects per project:
- **Nitrogen effect** (tonnes N) — hydrologially correct, per sub-catchment
- **Lowland extraction area** (ha) — based on Kulstof2022 overlap logic
- **Afforestation area** (ha) — including "urørt skov" (untouched forest)

Nature projects are tracked separately (no nitrogen contribution).

## Verification logic

SGAV's verification checks whether the combined projects in each local tripartite's plan meet the target for nitrogen reduction across the 37 coastal water groups. Nitrogen targets follow hydrology (108 sub-catchments → 37 coastal water groups), not administrative boundaries.

**Critical insight**: Nitrogen reduction targets cannot meaningfully be divided among municipalities or local tripartites as "goals" — they follow catchment hydrology. Municipalities/tripartites contribute, but goals are measured at the coastal water group level.

## Geographic responsibility mapping

- **4 water area districts** (vanddistrikter)
- **23 main catchments** (hovedvandoplande) = 23 local tripartites
- **37 hydrological coastal water groups** (kystvandgrupper) — the unit for measuring nitrogen targets
- **108 sub-catchments** (delopland) — VP3-II nitrogen targets distributed here
- **98 municipalities** — the implementers

## Feedback loops

- Mid-2026: status review of commitments in each local tripartite → determines nitrogen regulation stringency for 2027
- Municipalities must have updated project status in MARS for this review
- Taskforce meetings shift from "MARS data entry progress" (2025) to "implementation progress as measured in MARS" (2026+)
